01 Licence Chooser

Answer three questions to identify the right Creative Commons licence. All CC licences require attribution (BY) — the questions below determine what additional conditions apply.

Question 01 / 03
Do you want to allow commercial use of your work?
Commercial use means others can profit from using, sharing, or building on your work. If you restrict this, the NonCommercial (NC) condition applies.
Question 02 / 03
Do you want to allow others to adapt or build upon your work?
Derivatives include remixes, translations, and adaptations. The NoDerivatives (ND) condition prevents others from sharing modified versions — though personal-use adaptations are still permitted.
Question 03 / 03
Must adaptations carry the same licence (ShareAlike)?
ShareAlike (SA) is the copyleft condition — anyone who adapts and shares your work must release it under the same or a compatible licence. ND and SA cannot be combined.

02 Licence Elements

Every Creative Commons licence is built from combinations of four elements. Attribution (BY) is always required. The other three elements can be added as restrictions.

Openness spectrum — most permissive → most restrictive
CC0 · Public DomainCC BY-NC-ND · Most restricted

ND and SA are mutually exclusive — NoDerivatives prohibits sharing any adaptation, which is incompatible with the ShareAlike requirement. This reduces 16 theoretical combinations to six valid licences.
